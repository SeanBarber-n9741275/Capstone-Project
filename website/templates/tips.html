Resume Writing Tips

Below are some helpful tips to consider when writing your resume.

Use relevant keywords

Ensure that throughout your resume you are using key words that are relevant to the job you are applying for. Applicant tracking systems often rely heavily on key word searches to identify candidates to get reviewed by management.

Use keywords to highlight experiences

When an applicant tracking system flags a potential candidate, it will show management where it detected the keywords. By having your relevant experiences connected to the keywords, you will have a better chance of management reading them.

Keep it simple

Technical jargon will not often be listed within an applicant tracking systems keywords to search for. By keeping your terminology simple, you're not only making it easier for a human to read, but also an AI.

Do not use acronyms

This tip is very similar to keeping it simple. Applicant tracking systems often search for key words within a resume, but only rarely will they be calibrated to search for acronyms. To deal with this, define your acronyms when you must use them, as this will make it more clear for humans and applicant tracking systems.

Use the correct file type

If a business asks for a certain file type when submitting your resume online, make sure you use it. If you cannot, use .pdf or .docx documents, as they are the most commonly used. If you send an incompatible file type, they will be unable to process your resume.

Keep it as business related as possible

While showing your personal experiences may show good work ethic and leadership skills, be sure to limit these instances. Applicant tracking systems can develop biases based on previously accepted or rejected applicants. This means you could be blacklisted for something completely normal, just because the system has noticed people that have similar interests have failed the test before.
